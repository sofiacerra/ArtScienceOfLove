A project on love  ·  Honours Course: The Art & Science of Loving

What does love
look like to you?

A Google Form sent to friends and family. The only requirement was an image. What came back was a window into how the people I love see the world.

The project

I created a form inviting my close circle to share what love means to them through a photo and, optionally, a few words. Uploading an image was the only mandatory element since I was more interested in the visual language of love than in written definitions.

The project expanded organically when some friends chose to share it with their own network, showing how an act of love can ripple outward. So, even though I expected my target group to be my close social circle, it didn't stay only there.

It deepened my existing relationships: asking people to reflect on love opened conversations that went beyond the surface, making me experience genuine moments of connection and vulnerability. And it revealed something collective: many people instinctively chose images of home and physical spaces, pointing to a shared understanding of love as rooted in place and togetherness.

As it turns out, love doesn't need much of an invitation. It just needs a space to be expressed.

My reflection

What moves me most, looking at all of this together, is how many different shapes love takes and yet how each one circles back to the same quiet truth: love is presence. Whether it lives in a dog's eyes, a childhood village, a friend across borders, or a hand held through difficulty, love seems to be the act of making someone feel that they are not alone in the world.

The commitment people brought to their responses also moved me. Nobody gave a throwaway answer. Everyone paused. Everyone chose carefully. That care and that act of slowing down to think about love, felt to me like love itself.
